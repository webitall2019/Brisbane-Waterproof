Initial Waterproofing

Waterproofing is the process of making an object or structure waterproof or water-resistant so that it remains relatively unaffected by water or resisting the ingress of water under specified conditions.

Balcony Waterproofing

A balcony with minimal fall is susceptible to water pooling and moisture between the membrane and tiles, known as “ponding”. If the waterproofing membrane applied cannot withstand ponding water, over time the membrane will break down, causing water leaks.

Concrete waterproofing

Sealing concrete repels water and extends its life. If you have a concrete patio, garage, or driveway, sealing the concrete should be part of the initial conditioning of the surface, and should be done periodically over the life of the concrete.

Roof waterproofing

Roof waterproofing can help prevent standing water from creating leaks, shield the surface against ultraviolet light damage and can improve your home's energy efficiency.
